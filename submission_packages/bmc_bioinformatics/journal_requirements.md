# BMC Bioinformatics Requirements Notes

Official sources checked on 2026-03-29:

- Journal submission guidelines: <https://link.springer.com/journal/12859/submission-guidelines>
- BMC Bioinformatics database article guidance: <https://link.springer.com/journal/12859/submission-guidelines/database-article>

## Journal fit

- BMC Bioinformatics states that it considers novel computational algorithms and software, models and tools, including machine learning and AI, for analysis of biological data.
- BMC Bioinformatics also provides a dedicated `Database article` format.
- The database-article guidance states that if a manuscript describes a database and also includes research that merits publication in its own right, it should either be submitted as a research article or split into separate database and research papers.

## Package decision

- This package is prepared as a `Research article`.
- Reason: the current manuscript contains a database/KG resource contribution plus a substantial benchmark and ablation story.

## Key formatting and submission requirements

- Use editable source files; TeX/LaTeX is accepted.
- Use double-line spacing, include line and page numbering, and avoid page breaks in the manuscript file.
- Include an `Availability of data and materials` statement.
- Include a `Declarations` section with all BMC subheadings:
  - Ethics approval and consent to participate
  - Consent for publication
  - Availability of data and materials
  - Competing interests
  - Funding
  - Authors' contributions
  - Acknowledgements
  - Authors' information (optional)
- Cover letter should include:
  - why the manuscript fits BMC Bioinformatics
  - any policy issues
  - potential competing interests
  - confirmation that all authors approved submission
  - confirmation that the work is not published or under review elsewhere
- Reviewer suggestions/exclusions may be included in the cover letter.
- Data should be deposited in a public repository when community norms expect it; otherwise include as additional files or state access conditions.
- Tables should be editable, not embedded as images.
- Additional files can be uploaded, each up to 20 MB, and should be cited in sequence in the text.
