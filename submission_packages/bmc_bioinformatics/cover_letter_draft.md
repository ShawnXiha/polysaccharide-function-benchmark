# Cover Letter Draft for BMC Bioinformatics

Dear Editors of *BMC Bioinformatics*,

We are pleased to submit the manuscript entitled **"A DoLPHiN-Derived Polysaccharide Knowledge Graph for Interpretable Function Retrieval and Tail-Sensitive Ontology Propagation"** for consideration as a Research Article in *BMC Bioinformatics*.

This work transforms DoLPHiN into a typed polysaccharide knowledge graph and uses the resulting resource to study masked function retrieval, clean function-prediction diagnostics, and ontology-enhanced long-tail behavior. We believe the manuscript fits *BMC Bioinformatics* because it combines a reusable biological data resource with computational benchmarking, interpretable retrieval baselines, graph-based methodological analysis, and a structured supplementary package.

The manuscript makes three main contributions. First, it constructs a DoLPHiN-derived heterogeneous knowledge graph with explicit organism, monosaccharide, glycosidic bond, function, disease, and publication evidence. Second, it shows that under a clean unified split, explicit shallow graph-feature models are substantially stronger than the current hetero GNN baselines, and that message-passing ablations indicate a representation bottleneck rather than a simple tuning deficit. Third, it demonstrates that confidence-gated parent/child ontology propagation provides a stable tail-sensitive gain in a disease-aware upper-bound setting.

We are submitting this work as a Research Article rather than a Database Article because, in addition to the resource construction, the manuscript contains a substantial standalone benchmark and methodological evaluation. This choice is aligned with the journal's database-article guidance for submissions that combine a database description with research results meriting publication in their own right.
